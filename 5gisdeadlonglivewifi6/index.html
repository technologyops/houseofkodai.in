# 5G is dead: long live WiFi6 --- ## What do users care about ? * 24x7 reliable, fast, **Internet** connectivity. + first for their mobiles. + then for other connected devices: laptops, smart-tv, IoT, etc. * cost - lower is always better. ## But, what about voice calls ? * Voice over 2G/3G/LTE is facing the same fate as land-line calls. * Smart-phones have blurred the distinction between normal voice-calls and voice-over-internet calls. Users use apps to make calls. * Chat+Voice+Video over the internet is the new norm. ## Telecom Network Architecture * Fibre-backbone with wireless for last-mile connectivity. * LTE cell-site coverage has reduced to 300m ! * 5G will reduce the cell-site coverage area even more. ## Fibre-internet + last-mile WiFi6 * Will the users notice the difference ? * Does the "intentional complexity" of the existing telecom networks warrant a higher price ? ## WhyFi6 ? * Licensed spectrum is an artificial barrier. * Spectral efficiency is the key. * WiFi6 techniques such as OFDMA, and MU-MIMO have finally brought feature parity with LTE/5G. * A single WiFi6 access point can provide internet bandwidth to 100s of simultaneous stations, **today**. ## Technology evolution * Traditionally telecom networks had a technology shelf life in decades. * Then came mobile - which brought it down from 10 to 5 years (2G,3G,4G). * Now with 5G and beyond, they will need to adopt to a much shorter upgrade cycle. * WiFi7 will be out sooner than 5G rollout is complete ! ## Internet Economy * What people use are the _only_ standards that matter. * Users want things that work _now_. * Internet feedback is faster. * 100 million WiFi6 devices were released before the official standard was ratified ! * Organizations that move at internet speed will thrive, others will perish faster than they hope! ## A simple network * WiFi6 Mesh * fibre-internet-gateways * IPv6 only ## The nano ISP ! * Anybody with a fibre-internet connection can become an ISP ! Needs: * Incentives, financial/other, to manage and share Internet bandwidth. * Plug-n-deploy with automated cloud managed configuration/upgrades. ## The Challenges * WiFi6 is currently available only on flagship mobiles, costing about US $400/- 3-5 years is the estimate, when WiFi6 enabled mobile phones will reach price point of US $100/- and attain critical-mass. * Software engineering to provide a simple/immediate User eXperience. * Telecom companies who have invested a lot in retaining their oligopoly. * Regulatory. ## What next ? That's the trillion $ question :-D While I do see this as a disruptive technology - it's success in the marketplace probably depends more upon the political/social challenges. So far, even big-tech: Apple, Google, Facebook, Microsoft, Amazon, etc. have steered clear of entering this segment.
